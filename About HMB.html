What is HMB

Historical Medieval Battles or "HMB" is a fully armored, full contact fighting sport. The weapons and armor used in these fights are characteristic for the Middle Ages, that is anywhere between the 8th and 17th century. Fights take place in arenas known as "lists" which are of specific sizes and shapes, depending on the fight. All fights are moderated by a referee known as a "Knights Marshal". Although fighters wear suits of armor for protection, all weapons are blunted and do not have a cutting edge to them. HMB includes strikes that can be aimed at body parts, in compliance to the rules and regulations of the sport, and also include wrestling or tripping your opponent. Winners are judged by the given victory condition set by the category; Buhurt HMB for instance requires the 'removal' of a combatant or their team from the battle.

Fight Categories

Singles

Duels:

Duels are one on one fights that require a certain number of points, usually 10, to win the match. A point is gained whenever a deliberate strike hits an opponent.

  • Sword & Shield
  • Sword & Buckler
  • Longsword
  • Polearm
  • Triathlon: Three rounds with three different weapons

Pro-Fights:

Professional fights are held for three rounds, each of them 3 minuets long. In this match type grappling, tripping, and delivering blows to any part of the body is allowed expect for those restricted by the regulations.

 

Mass Combat

Mass combat includes two teams with an equal amount of fighters per team. The goal is to ground every fighter on the opposing team. This can be achieved in many ways including trips, knock outs, and wrestling manuvers. These fights can consist of teams ranging from 2 up to 150 (or more).

Regulations and Rules

HMB is a full contact sport that includes striking your opponent with steel weapons. As such, the sport has rules all fighters are obligated to follow. Those who break the rules are flagged by the Knight Marshals, the referees, and can include explusion from the match. Additonaly all fighters must be of age in terms of the law of thier country as well as the country hosting the event. Below are a few rules taken from the HMBIA website. https://hmbia.info/rules-and-regulations/

Allowed

  • Strikes to the back
  • Punches, kicks, shield bashes, knees
  • Hold an opponent in place
  • Strike an opponent who has lost their weapon
  • Strike an opponent who has lost balance until they hit the ground
  • Wrestling techniques such as throws and back-heels

Prohibited

  • Thrusts from weapons
  • Holding the edges of a helmet with hands
  • Holds that suffocate an opponent with hands or with weapons around their neck
  • Striking a fighter that is laying down or kneeling
  • Striking a fighter after they have lost a part of thier equipment
  • Striking an opponents eyes, back of the neck, back of the knees, groin or foot arches
Authenticity

Before a fighter is allowed to participate in an HMB event their arms and armor will be inspected and must comply with authenticity standards. These include analogues of a certain historical period, anywhere from the 8th to the 17th century, that can be confirmed by historical research. Protective arms must be made with this in mind which includes the materials used in the construction like metal, leather, and fabric components. Authenticity is measured by adherence to historical arms and armor used during the intended period of the reconstructed item. Weapons and armor that fail to meet authenticity standards are not allowed to be used in HMB.
